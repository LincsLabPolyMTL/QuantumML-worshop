About the Workshop

Where Quantum Computing Meets Machine Learning

This one-day workshop brings together researchers and practitioners exploring the intersection of quantum computing and machine learning — with a focus on two high-impact application domains: network anomaly detection and time-series forecasting.

Attendees will hear recent results on quantum reservoir computing, quantum recurrent networks, gate-based models, and AI-assisted circuit optimization, with applications to time series analysis and network anomaly detection.

Key Topics

Research Areas

The workshop covers a broad spectrum of quantum ML techniques applied to networking and forecasting.

⚛️

Quantum Reservoir Computing

Gate-based and dynamical reservoir models for classification and time-series learning tasks.

🔁

Quantum Recurrent Networks

Quantum analogues of recurrent gated architectures (LSTM/GRU) for sequential data.

🛡️

Network Anomaly Detection

Applying quantum ML to identify anomalies and intrusions in real-time network traffic.

📈

Time-Series Forecasting

Quantum kernel and circuit-based methods for multi-step prediction on temporal data.

✂️

AI-based Circuit Cutting

Leveraging AI to partition large quantum circuits for execution on near-term hardware.

🌀

Generative Quantum Models

Quantum-enhanced latent generative models for anomaly synthesis and data augmentation.
